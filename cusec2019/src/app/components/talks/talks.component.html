Jin Guo's talk

@Jin_LC_Guo

Supporting and Utilizing Software Traceability

Modern Software and Systems engineering projects produce large quantities of data (artifacts) as a natural by-product of the engineering process. These artifacts, including user stories, requirements, design documents, source code, and test cases, are interconnected, and their connectivity can be used to answer questions of both the software product and its development process. The connections between software artifacts are represented as trace links. Creating trace links manually is time-consuming and error-prone and several studies have shown that it produces incomplete, redundant, and inaccurate links. In this talk, I will explain several ways to achieve automated accurate trace link generation through machine learning and natural language processing techniques. I will also illustrate how trace links can deliver actionable intelligence in support of software engineering activities such as knowledge base construction, impact analysis, and project Q&A.

Matthieu Courbariaux's talk

Towards neuromorphic hardware

Training and running deep neural networks is very computationally intensive. The performance of our algorithms was and still is to a large extent limited by the throughput of our computing hardware. Many Deep Learning breakthroughs were enabled by a change in hardware (e.g. from CPU to GPU), a better use of existing hardware, or even Moore's law. However, we are currently reaching the physical limits to transistor scaling, and Moore's law is likely coming to an end. As a result, neuromorphic hardware, i.e. brain-like computers, are gaining more and more traction. The goal of this presentation is to give an overview of the past, present and future of Deep Learning hardware.

Kathy Giori's talk

@kgiori

Mozilla's Internet of Things (IoT) Framework Puts You in Control

Typical industry approaches to IoT teach the approach of bridging device data to the cloud. In such cases, end-users have to download numerous smartphone apps, each with its own subscriber account. Mixing and matching across brands is typically hard. If an IoT startup goes out of business, early-adopter customers end up with useless gear. Mozilla's approach is to break down proprietary silo's and decentralize IoT, putting data in the hands of users. Our open source Things Gateway implementation bridges IoT data to the web. Our numerous open source libraries in several languages help drive a standards-based web of things approach. Come learn how to build and operate your own private smart home, using the Mozilla IoT framework.

Olivier Bilodeau's talk

@obilodeau

Cybersecurity from the Trenches

The field of Cybersecurity can appear intimidating at first. It is a field of computer science that is adversarial by definition: protecting assets against enemies. What makes it worse, defenders need to get everything right, one mistake is enough for adversaries to compromise an organization. Lastly, everyone in that field on Twitter takes themselves so seriously... This presentation will explore what cybersecurity has to offer from a software engineering perspective. Reverse-engineering, malware analysis and research, intrusion testing (aka ethical hacking), forensics and incident response are all opportunities that are interesting in themselves or that require solid tooling that engineers, with knowledge of cybersecurity, needs to build. Punctuated by stories and anecdotes, we might demonstrate that "Cyberwar is a thing" and this might be just why we need an influx of talented engineers in that field right now.
